Hard Time 2000-03-25

Pictures Below

This show started out with Dustin Credible reviewing the card. Because Victor E. Moore was suspended by the ECWF Executive committee Credible was out of a match. The Executive committee decided to make a triple threat match for the ECWF North American Title. It was the champion Assman versus Russ Rage versus Dustin Credible. This was an elimination Triple Threat Match. Russ Rage was mad and wanted the title and the match started off with him on offense. Then the Assman was about to pedigree Credible but Rage came in and there was a double pedigree on Credible. The match continued on when Credible low blow'd the other two superstars. Credible got up on the ladder and executed a Centon Bomb on Assman for the pin. Rage and Credible were the only two left. While Credible was performing his move, Rage was getting his breath back. Right after the Centon Bomb, Rage came in and gave Credible the Sexy Slam for the 1, 2, 3, and there is a new ECWF North American Champion.

Now it was time for the much anticipated Table Death Match 2000. This was for the ECWF Heavyweight Title between champion Christopher Winner and The Franchise Jake Humphrey. This was a match for the ages with both superstars performing some of there signature moves. Winner snap suplexed Humphrey and was about to put him on the table but Humphrey evened the score with an eye gouge. He then proceeded to do a deadly power-bomb onto Winner. While Humphrey showed off to the camera Winner hit him with a deadly low blow. Winner put Humphrey on the table and climbed the ladder. When Winner was on top of the ladder, Humphrey regained his strength and pushed the ladder off from under him. Humphrey put Winner on the table, climbed the ladder, and executed the five star frog splash on Winner through the table. He was the new ECWF Heavyweight Champion. After the match Russ Rage came in and started beating on Winner. He declared a new era in the ECWF. The Rage-Franchise era. It is positive that Rage and Franchise have all the gold in the ECWF.
